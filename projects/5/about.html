Urban Radical

BE HAPPY!

Chicago-based street artist keeping the streets happy!

Starting off in the summer of 2015, Urban Radical has been spreading the message of "Be Happy!" among the streets of Chicago in various neighborhoods.

Originally starting off by copying the works of Penny Pinch, a well-known Chicago street artist, Urban Radical got into street art by exploring various Chicago neighborhoods and studying the craft by watching YouTube videos and researching. He adopts his style from artists like Keith Haring, Jean-Michel Basquiat, and Hebru Brantley.

Influences Through Music

Inspiration for Urban Radical's work comes from listening to music, mainly hip-hop and R&B, exploring in and out of Chicago, and various day-to-day activities, such as riding the train and hanging with friends.

Influences Through Art

Urban Radical style stems off of different styles from a variety of artists, either adapting their style in a similar way or changing the layout of how their work appears. His favorite artists include Keith Haring, Jean-Michel Basquiat, Hebru Brantley, and Sentrock in terms of visual art and digital work.
